Our Signature Products

Sweets

Gulab Jamun: Small, round dough balls made from milk solids (khoya), deep-fried and soaked in a sweet syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water, offering a soft, melt-in-the-mouth texture.

Jalebi: A crispy, coil-shaped sweet made from a batter of flour and yogurt, deep-fried and soaked in sugar syrup, known for its crunchy exterior and syrupy, juicy interior.

Imarti: Similar to Jalebi, but made from urad dal flour, deep-fried into intricate spirals, and soaked in sugar syrup, giving it a unique texture and a slightly different taste.

Savouries

Indian savouries are a delightful range of crispy and spicy snacks enjoyed across the country. These include:

Namkeen: A mix of salty, crispy treats like Aloo Bhujia and Sev.
Chakli/Murukku: Spiral-shaped snacks made from rice and lentil flour.
Mathri: Flaky, spiced crackers perfect for tea-time.
Banana Chips: Thin, salted or spiced fried banana slices.
Khakhra: Crisp, flavored flatbreads made from whole wheat.
Kachori: Deep-fried rounds stuffed with lentils or spices.

Batter

Indian food batters form the base for a variety of traditional dishes, offering convenience and authenticity. Here's a list of popular batter specialties:

Idli Batter: Made from fermented rice and urad dal, this batter produces soft, fluffy steamed rice cakes called idlis.
Dosa Batter: A slightly thinner version of idli batter, perfect for making crispy, golden crepes known as dosas.
Medu Vada Batter: A spiced urad dal batter used to make crispy, donut-shaped fritters called medu vadas.
